About Me

I'm a former community organizer turned developer.

Witnessing first-hand tech's potential to create massive social impact drove me to dig deep and learn how to build applications that could make the world a better and more equitable place.

I led up logistics and held leadership roles in the nonprofit sector for most of my early career, working with organizations dealing with immigrants' rights all the way to racial justice. I enjoyed designing and solving technical problems for great causes so much that I decided to branch out as a full-time freelance web and graphic designer.

I recently graduated from a coding bootcamp in Silicon Valley, where I spent several months learning how to speak JavaScript and building MEAN (MongoDB, Express, Angular, Node) stack apps from front to back from scratch.

When I am not fiddling with code or prototyping something, I like to read, write, and doodle. I am also a published writer and my illustrations have appeared here.
